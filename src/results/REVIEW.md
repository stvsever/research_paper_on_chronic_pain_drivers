# Review guide

This file is a map for reviewing the analysis. It lists the claims in the manuscript and the
result files that support each one. All numbers are regenerated by the pipeline; nothing is
hand-typed into the manuscript.

## Sample and design

- 68 analytic participants, 6,262 completed prompts, median 96 per person (range 30 to 122);
  65 of 68 completed at least 50 prompts. See `tables/02_timing_summary.csv`; the
  per-person compliance export is kept local because it is participant-level.
- Baseline characteristics: `tables/02_person_level_table1.csv` (Table 1).

## RQ1: within-person threat-attention-pain circuit

- Core nodes: pain (PIJN), threat value (THREAT = mean of momentary fear and catastrophizing),
  attention to pain (ATTEND), goal engagement (ENGAGE).
- Within-person variance justifies temporal modelling: `tables/03_variance_decomposition.csv`.
- Pooled benchmark temporal fixed effects: `networks/05_mlvar_core_temporal_edges.csv`
  (Table `tab:temporal`). Circuit edges: threat to attention, threat to pain, pain to attention,
  attention to pain, and pain to engagement.
- Contemporaneous and between-person networks: `networks/05_mlvar_core_contemporaneous_edges.csv`,
  `networks/05_mlvar_core_between_edges.csv`.
- Per-person heterogeneity: `networks/07_perperson_edge_heterogeneity.csv`; per-person coupling
  exports are kept local because they contain participant-level estimates.
- Regularized individual networks: `networks/07_graphicalvar_edge_selection_freq.csv`; per-person
  individual-network summaries are kept local.
- Confirmatory S-GIMME: `networks/06_sgimme_summary_path_counts.csv`; subgroup membership is kept
  local because it is participant-level.

## RQ2: baseline profiles and the momentary experience of pain

- Trait profiles (threat, biomedical, personality): participant-level profile exports are kept
  local.
- Between-person associations of baseline profiles with mean momentary states:
  `tables/09_between_person_correlations.csv` (Table `tab:link`),
  `tables/09_between_person_hierarchical.csv`.
- Cross-level moderation (one-step, random slopes): `tables/09_moderation.csv`
  (Table `tab:sup-moderation`).

## Robustness and diagnostics

- Detrended, attention composite, stricter compliance, extended six-node, threat-component
  decomposition: the `robust_*` and `extended_*` files in `networks/`.
- Leave-one-out and bootstrap: `networks/robust_loo_summary.csv`,
  `networks/robust_bootstrap_summary.csv` (Table `tab:robust`).
- Residual diagnostics: `tables/05_mlvar_residual_diagnostics.csv`.
- Daily dysfunction: `tables/11_daily_dysfunction_models.csv`.

## Provenance and scope

- The data were collected by Ilse Viane within FWO project G.0032.01 (Crombez and De Corte).
- The analysis focuses on the cognitive-affective model of the interruptive function of pain and
  does not use acceptance as an analytic node.
